Meet the CMS-1500 Form

What is the CMS-1500?

The CMS-1500 (also called HCFA-1500) is the standard paper claim form used to bill Medicare, Medicaid, and most commercial payers for professional (non-facility) services.

Current version: CMS-1500 (02/12)

Even though most claims are now electronic, the CMS-1500 is the visual standard that maps to the electronic 837P transaction.

Three Main Sections

Boxes 1–13
Patient & Insurance
Who is the patient? Who pays?
Boxes 14–24
Clinical & Services
What happened? What was done?
Boxes 25–33
Provider & Billing
Who did it? Where?

Clinical & Service Blocks (14–24)

The Heart of the Claim

Boxes 14–24 tell the payer what happened clinically and what services were performed. This is where medical necessity is demonstrated.

Key Blocks

Box 14: Date of Illness/Injury +
The date the condition started. Source: provider note. Format: MM/DD/YYYY.
Box 17: Referring Provider +
Name of the referring/ordering provider (if applicable).
Box 21: Diagnosis Codes +
ICD-10-CM codes (up to 12) that justify the services. Labeled A–L.
Box 24: Service Lines +
24A Date of Service, 24B Place of Service, 24D CPT code, 24E Diagnosis Pointer, 24F Charges, 24G Units.

📄 Sample Provider Note

DOS: 03/10/2025   POS: Office (11)

CC: Sore throat and fever for 3 days

History: Also has hypertension (controlled) and seasonal allergies. Smokes 1 pack/day.

Assessment: Acute pharyngitis (J02.9)

Provider & Billing Blocks (25–33)

Boxes 25–33 identify the provider, practice, and billing entity. Source: provider office records.

Key Blocks

Box 25: Federal Tax ID (EIN) +
Practice or provider's EIN or SSN. Check the correct box.
Box 27: Accept Assignment +
Yes = provider accepts the payer's allowed amount as payment in full.
Box 31: Provider Signature +
Rendering provider signature and date. "Signature on File" for electronic.
Box 32: Service Facility +
Name and address where the service was performed.
Box 33: Billing Provider & NPI +
Name, address, phone, and NPI of the billing provider/practice.
